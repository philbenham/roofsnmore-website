What we install

Two sizes. Three metals. Thirty-two colors.

5-inch K-style aluminum

Standard for most Houston homes. Moves plenty of water for single-story roofs.

  • .027 or .032 gauge aluminum
  • Hidden hangers (no spikes)
  • Baked-on Kynar finish

6-inch K-style aluminum

For bigger roofs, two-stories, or areas with heavy rain. Holds ~40% more water.

  • .032 gauge aluminum
  • 3×4 downspouts (vs 2×3)
  • Same 32 color options

Half-round copper / steel

Upgrade pick for traditional and craftsman homes. Ages into a beautiful patina.

  • 16-oz copper or galvanized steel
  • Soldered joints
  • Custom brackets
How gutter install works

Start at 8am. Done by dark.

1

Measure & quote

We measure every roofline, count corners and downspouts, and send a written price in 24 hours.

2

Samples come to your house. Most people match the trim or the fascia. 5" or 6" depends on roof area.

Common questions

Gutter questions we get every spring.

5-inch or 6-inch — which do I need?

5-inch is standard for most Houston homes. Go 6-inch if your home is over 2,500 sq ft or has a steep pitch — it holds about 40% more water. We'll tell you exactly what your roof needs when we measure.

What does gutter installation cost?

Most Houston homes run $1,500–$3,500 depending on linear footage, size, and whether you add guards. Aluminum lasts 20–30 years; copper systems last 50+ years. We give you a written price before we start.

Are micro-mesh guards worth it in Houston?

Yes — especially in tree-dense neighborhoods. Stainless steel micro-mesh guards keep oak leaves, pine needles, and debris out while letting Houston's heavy rain flow through freely. They pay for themselves in avoided cleanings within a few years.

Why do gutters matter so much in Houston?

At 52 inches of rain per year, every drop that doesn't drain away properly finds its own path — usually straight at your foundation. Houston's clay soil expands and contracts dramatically with moisture, making proper drainage critical to protecting your slab.
